## Question 7: Vision Gaps & Uncertainties

> *If anything, what do you think is missing in the vision today, and which aspects are you the most uncertain about?*

### What's Missing in the Current Vision

#### 1. The "How We Win" is Underdeveloped

The vision articulates a compelling "what" (autonomous spend management) and "why" (CFO transformation), but the competitive differentiationâ€”the "how we win against specific competitors"â€”needs sharpening. What is the wedge that creates initial traction? What prevents Tipalti, BILL, or SAP from copying our moves?

**Recommendation:** Develop explicit competitive positioning for each major competitor segment. Identify the 2-3 proof points that make our claims credible.

#### 2. The SMB Strategy is Absent

The vision focuses on enterprise CFO transformation, but AI-native entrants (Ramp, Brex, Mercury) are winning SMB and moving upmarket. If Medius cedes SMB, we lose the supplier network effects and talent pipeline that come from high-volume customer acquisition.

**Recommendation:** Add a Product-Led Growth pillar. Define a free or low-cost tier that captures SMB while generating supplier network value and enterprise leads.

#### 3. The Partner Strategy is Implicit

For a platform vision to succeed, ecosystem matters. The current vision doesn't address: Which partners extend our value? Where do we integrate vs. build? How do we become essential to the tech stack rather than replaceable?

**Recommendation:** Develop explicit partner strategy: ERP integration depth, contract management partnerships (Ironclad), tax compliance (Avalara), and potentially banking/fintech partnerships for payment monetization.

#### 4. The AI Safety & Trust Narrative is Missing

As AI becomes autonomous, customers will worry about errors, liability, and control. The vision should proactively address: How do we ensure AI decisions are explainable? What's the human override model? How do we handle mistakes? This is table stakes for enterprise adoption of autonomous systems.

**Recommendation:** Add "Trustworthy AI" as a principle. Develop the explainability, audit, and override framework that gives CFOs confidence to let AI execute.

### Areas of Greatest Uncertainty

#### 1. Pace of AI Capability Advancement

The AI landscape is moving faster than any technology shift I've seen. What's differentiating today (custom models, domain data) may be commoditized by foundation models in 18-24 months. Our strategy assumes domain expertise + AI creates durable advantage, but this may compress faster than expected.

**Mitigation:** Build strategic optionality. Ensure architecture can adopt new foundation models quickly. Focus on data and workflow moats that persist regardless of underlying AI technology.

#### 2. Payment Monetization Economics

The payment monetization bet assumes we can achieve meaningful scale in virtual cards, dynamic discounting, and SCF. But these require supplier adoption, buyer cash availability, and competitive rebate economics. The unit economics are uncertain at Medius scale.

**Mitigation:** Start with design partners. Test economics with 10-20 committed customers before major investment. Build partnerships with established payment networks rather than going fully proprietary.

#### 3. Organizational Readiness for Transformation

Executing this vision requires significant changeâ€”new capabilities, new go-to-market motions, new metrics. After 20 years as an AP automation company, can Medius's culture, talent, and processes transform fast enough? Organizational inertia is often the binding constraint.

**Mitigation:** Invest in change management. Hire transformation experience. Create isolated teams for new bets to protect from antibodies. Celebrate early wins aggressively.

#### 4. Marlin's Investment Horizon & Appetite

PE ownership creates both opportunity (capital for transformation) and constraint (timeline to exit). The strategic bets proposed here require 18-36 month investment horizons. Does the ownership structure support this? What's the exit thesis, and does this strategy serve it?

**Mitigation:** Align strategy to exit timeline. Sequence bets so early wins (e-invoicing, payment monetization) generate returns while platform bets (agentic AI) build strategic value. Frame investments in terms that resonate with financial sponsors.
